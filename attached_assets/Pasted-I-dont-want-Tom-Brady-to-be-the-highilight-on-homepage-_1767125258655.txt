Why it can backfire:

Distracts from the product

"What does football have to do with my business?"
"Is this just a gimmick?"


Limits your market

Not everyone likes football
International customers don't know Tom Brady
Some industries are formal/serious (finance, healthcare, government)


Ages poorly

Brady retires → reference feels dated
Different QB rises → your positioning is stuck in the past




THE BETTER APPROACH: PRINCIPLE-LED POSITIONING
Use the mindset. Ditch the celebrity.
The preparation philosophy is universal. It doesn't need a famous face to validate it.

## WHERE TO KEEP THE FOOTBALL ANALOGY

**Use it strategically in these places:**

### 1. **About Page / Founder Story**
"I spent 5 years coaching major college football. I watched coaches coordinate 11 players in 40 seconds using pre-staged playbooks. Then I spent 20 years in Fortune 500 companies watching executives take 72 hours to coordinate 5 people via email. The sideline was better prepared than the boardroom. That's why I built M Platform."

### 2. **Sales Pitch (When Appropriate)**
Read the room. If they're football fans, use the Brady analogy. If not, use the universal preparation principles.

### 3. **Internal Culture**
Use football terminology internally:
- "Film Room" for post-execution review
- "Two-Minute Drill" for urgent execution mode
- "Depth Chart" for stakeholder mapping
- "Game Day" for live execution

But don't force it on customers who don't resonate with it.
