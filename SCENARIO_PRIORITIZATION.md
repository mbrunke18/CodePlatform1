## Prioritization Matrix

### Evaluation Criteria (1-5 scale)
- **Executive Pain**: How acute is the problem?
- **ROI Clarity**: Can we measure success quickly?
- **Implementation Speed**: How fast to production?
- **Universal Applicability**: Works for all Fortune 1000?
- **Competitive Urgency**: Why now vs. later?
- **Integration Complexity**: How many systems to connect?

## THE DECISION: Crisis Management

### Why This Is The Right First Move

#### ‚úÖ **For Design Partners**
- "Prepare for your next crisis" is an easy sell
- Can simulate crisis in pilot without waiting for real event
- Fast time-to-value (90 days vs. 12+ months)
- Low risk (tabletop exercise vs. live production system)

#### ‚úÖ **For Bastion**
- Proves the entire platform concept: Prepare ‚Üí Monitor ‚Üí Execute ‚Üí Learn
- Showcases AI value: Alert detection, stakeholder coordination, learning from outcomes
- Creates urgency: "Next crisis is coming, are you ready?"
- Generates word-of-mouth: Crisis stories spread fast among executives

#### ‚úÖ **For Investors**
- Clear category definition: "Crisis Coordination Platform"
- Obvious market need: Every Fortune 1000 = 500 potential customers
- Expansion path: Start with crisis, expand to other scenarios
- Defensible moat: Institutional memory = competitive advantage

## 90-Day Execution Plan: Crisis Management MVP

### Phase 1: Design Partner Acquisition (Weeks 1-2)
**Goal**: Sign 3 design partners

**Target Companies**:
- Recent crisis experience (PR disaster, security breach, executive departure)
- 5,000-50,000 employees (large enough to have complexity)
- Progressive culture (willing to try new tech)

**Pitch**:
> "Your last crisis cost you $18M and took 72 hours to coordinate. We'll help you prepare for the next one and respond in under 4 hours. Free 90-day pilot in exchange for reference."

**Outreach Strategy**:
1. LinkedIn: Target COOs, Chiefs of Staff, VP Corporate Comms at companies with recent news coverage
2. Network: Ask existing contacts for warm intros
3. Industry Events: CHRO/COO conferences, crisis management summits

---

### Phase 2: Crisis Playbook Hardening (Weeks 3-6)
**Goal**: Production-ready crisis coordination workflow

**Build**:
1. **Crisis Detection**:
   - Social media monitoring (Twitter/Reddit API)
   - News alerts (Google News API)
   - Internal reporting system (email/Slack webhook)
   
2. **Stakeholder Coordination**:
   - Automated war room creation (Slack channel + Zoom link)
   - Stakeholder notification (email + SMS)
   - Role assignment (RACI matrix automation)
   
3. **Task Execution**:
   - Jira integration (auto-create crisis response tickets)
   - Status dashboard (real-time progress tracking)
   - Communication templates (pre-approved messaging)
   
4. **Outcome Tracking**:
   - Timeline analysis (how fast was response?)
   - Cost tracking (how much did coordination cost?)
   - Learning capture (what worked? what didn't?)

**Integration Priority**:
- **Week 3**: Slack + Email (communication)
- **Week 4**: Google Calendar + Zoom (coordination)
- **Week 5**: Jira + Asana (task management)
- **Week 6**: Custom dashboard (reporting)

---

### Phase 3: Pilot Execution (Weeks 7-10)
**Goal**: 3 design partners complete crisis simulations

**Pilot Protocol**:
1. **Week 7**: Playbook workshop with each design partner
   - Define their top 3 crisis scenarios
   - Build stakeholder matrix (who needs to know?)
   - Create communication templates
   
2. **Week 8**: Crisis simulation #1 (tabletop exercise)
   - Inject fictional crisis (e.g., "Data breach discovered")
   - Measure response time, coordination efficiency
   - Capture learnings
   
3. **Week 9**: Refine playbook based on simulation feedback
   - Update stakeholder lists
   - Improve communication templates
   - Fix integration bugs
   
4. **Week 10**: Crisis simulation #2 (real-time drill)
   - More realistic scenario
   - Measure improvement vs. simulation #1
   - Prepare success story

---

## Risk Mitigation

### What If Design Partners Don't Sign?
**Backup Plan**: Offer even more value
- Free forever for first 3 customers (not just 90 days)
- Dedicated implementation support (40 hours)
- Co-development: They help define the product

### What If Crisis Playbook Takes Longer Than 6 Weeks?
**Backup Plan**: Simplify ruthlessly
- Start with just Slack + Email integration (no Jira)
- Manual task tracking (no automation)
- Prove coordination value first, automate later

### What If No Crises Happen During Pilot?
**This is actually fine!**
- Simulations are sufficient to prove value
- "Preparedness" is the sell, not "response"
- Better positioning: "Hope for best, prepare for worst"
